🚲 Moving Archer Forward: Dueling Rallies, Editorial Fire, and Compromise on the Table

The Moving Archer Forward complete streets plan has become the most contentious infrastructure debate on Chicago’s Southwest Side. For over 4 months, weekly dueling rallies have drawn hundreds to Archer Avenue — bike safety advocates from Bike Grid Now! on one side, and the Archer Guardians (led by 12th Ward aldermanic challenger Claudia Zuno) on the other. The Chicago Tribune Editorial Board weighed in on May 1 with a call for “sensible compromise,” while Ald. Julia Ramirez (12th) signed off on edits to the plan in late April. Key concessions include preserving more parking and adjusting lane configurations. The Chicago Department of Planning and Development held its second public meeting March 1 at McKinley Park Fieldhouse, emphasizing a pedestrian-friendly main street vision. The project remains in active planning with no final CDOT approval yet.
