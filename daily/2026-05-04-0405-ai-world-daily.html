This Week in AI

  • 1. Anthropic nears $1.5B joint venture with Blackstone, Goldman Sachs for AI tools
    Anthropic, Blackstone and Hellman & Friedman are anchoring the deal (~$300M each). Goldman Sachs is investing ~$150M. The JV will develop and sell AI tools to private equity-backed companies using a Palantir-style consulting model.
    LINKS: Reuters • GuruFocus
  • 2. Pentagon inks AI deals with 7 tech companies — Anthropic excluded
    The Pentagon reached agreements with Amazon Web Services, Google, Microsoft, OpenAI, SpaceX, NVIDIA, Reflection, and Oracle to deploy their AI on classified military networks. Anthropic was notably absent due to its legal dispute over safety guardrails and mass surveillance concerns.
    LINKS: AP News • Reuters • The Guardian
  • 3. Critical MCP security flaw exposes 200,000 AI servers to RCE
    OX Security researchers uncovered an architectural RCE vulnerability in Anthropic's Model Context Protocol, affecting 150M+ downloads and up to 200K server instances. Anthropic declined to patch, calling the behavior 'expected'. Multiple CVEs issued; LiteLLM, Flowise, Bisheng patched. Windsurf and LangChain-Chatchat still exposed.
    LINKS: VentureBeat • The Register • The Hacker News
  • 4. AI outperforms doctors in Harvard emergency triage trial
    Researchers tested an AI model against physicians in diagnosing hospital patients, and the model outperformed the humans. The study suggests a future 'triadic care model' of doctor, patient, and AI working together.
    LINKS: NPR • The Guardian
  • 5. Big Tech AI spending set to hit $600B — investors question payoff
    Big Tech has spent hundreds of billions over three years powering the AI boom. Meta alone could spend $145B this year on AI. Goldman Sachs says the AI software sell-off was overdone, calling it a buying opportunity.
    LINKS: Reuters • Gizmodo • Motley Fool
  • 6. Bi-partisan AI worry unites Democrats and Republicans
    Growing unease over AI is one of the few things uniting Americans across the political spectrum. 70% believe AI will lead to fewer job opportunities, up from 56% a year ago. China, meanwhile, embraces AI while Americans distrust it.
    LINKS: NYT • Washington Post
  • 7. Chinese court rules companies can't fire workers just because AI is cheaper
    A Chinese court ruled that automation alone doesn't justify layoffs — a landmark decision as China both leads in AI adoption and grapples with its workforce implications.
